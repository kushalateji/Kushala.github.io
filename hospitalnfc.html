Hospital Data Management

Introduction

Modernizing healthcare data management is essential for enhancing patient care and optimizing hospital operations. The integration of advanced technologies, such as Near Field Communication (NFC), with medical history management presents a promising solution. This research focuses on implementing a Hospital Patient Medical History Management System using NFC-enabled wristbands, providing an efficient and secure approach to storing and accessing patients' vital information.

Key Aspects

  • NFC-Enabled Wristbands: The utilization of waterproof and reusable silicone wristbands embedded with encrypted NFC chips revolutionizes traditional patient identification and medical record systems.
  • Efficient Data Retrieval: NFC technology allows for quick and secure data retrieval, enabling healthcare providers to access comprehensive patient medical histories on the go, enhancing the speed and accuracy of diagnosis and treatment.
  • Paperless Information Handling: The transition from paper-based medical records to electronic data storage contributes to a reduction in physical storage requirements, eliminates the risk of loss or damage, and fosters eco-friendly, paperless information handling.
  • Structured Storage of Documents: Electronic storage facilitates structured organization of patient documents, including previous ailments, comorbidity details, prognosis, and allergies. This structured approach aids doctors in obtaining a comprehensive understanding of the patient's health history.

Development Process

The development process of the Hospital Patient Medical History Management System involves several key stages:

  • Patient Categorization

    Patients are categorized as either INPATIENT or OUTPATIENT at the front desk. NFC-enabled wristbands are specifically provided to INPATIENTS, facilitating comprehensive medical history management for those requiring immediate and long-term care.

  • Data Collection and Conversion

    INPATIENTS fill out a medical data form, capturing both basic and detailed information. The collected data is stored electronically in the hospital's cloud database and converted into an exclusive Hypertext link assigned to each patient ID.

  • Hospital Cloud Database

    The designated URL, containing individual patient data, is stored in the hospital's cloud database. This serves as a read/write module and a secure backup, ensuring data accessibility even after the patient leaves the hospital.

  • NFC Encoding and Encryption

    NFC wristbands are encoded with the unique Hypertext link using NFC readers. Encryption with a custom username and password adds an additional layer of security, allowing authorized personnel to access patient data.

  • Diagnosis and Report Update

    Medical reports, including prognosis, scans, X-rays, ultrasounds, MRIs, and drug prescriptions, are updated to the NFC wristband and the hospital cloud database after each doctor visit, providing real-time information to the patient.

  • Patient Exit Procedure

    Upon receiving an exit letter, the patient's NFC wristband is collected, sterilized, and formatted for reusability. This ensures the wristbands are safe for the next inpatient, and all data is securely erased.

  • Electronic Data Retrieval and Reusability

    Electronic data stored in the hospital database allows patients to easily retrieve their medical history during subsequent visits. The process supports continuity of care and facilitates a smooth transition to new healthcare providers.
